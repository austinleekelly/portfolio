## Austin Kelly | Manager, Operations | UX and Communications Copywriter  
Last Mile Delivery – Driver Engagement and Communications 

## Overview

This year I focused on improving the clarity, accuracy, and reliability of driver facing content while strengthening the operational foundations that support scale, automation, and lower cost to serve. I joined Walmart this year, so learning the culture, tools, and operating models while delivering meaningful work was a core part of my performance.

My work aligned closely with our cascading goals around style guide compliance, content clarity, lifecycle improvement, AI readiness, and knowledge modernization. 

While much of my work was driven by intake requests tied to new Spark Driver features and policies - I consistently looked beyond individual asks to identify patterns, risks, and opportunities to improve how content is created, reviewed, and maintained.

## Acting with Integrity Through Accuracy and Risk Awareness

I approached content work with a strong focus on accuracy, compliance, and agent understanding. 

I consistently followed the approved style guide and did not attempt to redefine or reinterpret it. Instead, I reduced risk by asking clarifying questions, chasing down answers across teams, and validating assumptions before publishing, even when it slowed delivery.

I used AI tools to double check style adherence, surface conflicting guidance across articles, and identify discrepancies that could lead to agent or driver confusion. 

I also used AI to rewrite and clarify content while maintaining approved patterns and terminology. This approach helped reduce errors and inconsistencies in high-risk content without introducing new compliance risk.

This work directly supports the goal of strong style guide adherence and risk mitigation, and reflects Walmart values around integrity, accountability, and transparency.

## Serving Drivers Through Clear, Reliable Content

Most of my content work was driven by intake requests from teams shipping new features in the Spark Driver app. Within that model, I focused on ensuring each update was clear, accurate, and easy for agents and drivers to understand.

I authored and updated content across high impact areas including pharmacy cold chain handling, reconstituted prescriptions, OTP based workflows, enrollment changes, login and password reset guidance, and application and background check transitions. These updates prioritized clarity and correctness over speed, particularly for sensitive workflows where errors could impact trust or safety.

While we do not consistently receive analytics on contact reduction, this work was designed to prevent confusion and reduce follow up questions by improving first pass understanding for agents and drivers.

## Improving Content Clarity Through UX Artifacts

To support contact reduction by design and upcoming channel changes, I created the Support Contact Points - Spark Driver Journey deck, consolidating all known support entry points across both in-app and web surfaces.

The deck:

- Provided a unified, end‑to‑end view of where drivers encounter support
    
- Surfaced inconsistencies, redundancies, and high‑risk friction points
    
- Included annotated UI screenshots with recommended updates
    
- Helped clarify ownership and next steps across product, UX, and operations teams
    

This deliverable was widely shared and became a quick reference for DX, Product, CES, Engineering, and Chatbot teams. 

Multiple leaders shared that it was clear, helpful, and immediately usable. This work helped teams align more quickly and supported broader efforts around phone number reduction and conversational automation.

## Strengthening Operations and Lifecycle Foundations

Beyond individual content updates, I focused on improving how content work is tracked, understood, and sustained.

I created an internal Airtable based resource hub that consolidated links, documentation, and tools I needed during my first months at Walmart. I shared this hub with subsequent hires and existing team members to improve onboarding and reduce repeated questions.

I also built a structured process improvement tracker that documented 25+ recommendations across content and workflow operations. These were prioritized by effort, impact, risk, and benefit, with clear statuses, ownership indicators, and filters for quick wins and items in motion. 

While I did not formally own implementation of all recommendations, a few reached an MVP state and created a foundation for future team adoption.

This work aligns with goals around continuous improvement, lifecycle optimization, and reducing outdated or conflicting content.

## Building AI Ready Knowledge Through CCA2 Workflow Visibility

One of my most impactful contributions was designing and implementing a reusable CCA2 workflow documentation process. 

I collaboratively pursued the idea and designed the full export/conversion process. After consulting Engineering to understand how to access workflow JSON, I created a reusable AI prompt and standardized outputs that convert CCA2 workflows into structured Word documents and spreadsheets, with optional diagrams.

This process:

- Is reusable without my direct involvement
    
- Is documented and accessible
    
- Is actively used for POCs requesting agent flows
    
- Was used to support Project Sierra and LMD chatbot automation
    
- Provides visibility to partners who do not have CCA2 access
    

Previously, documenting workflows required manual recreation in Figma and could take one to two hours per flow. With over 220 workflows, this created significant overhead and risk. The new process produces consistent outputs in minutes, reduces manual effort, and makes reviews clearer and more asynchronous.

Leadership and engineering partners described this work as innovative and a strong use of AI. This contribution supports goals around AI training readiness, workflow modernization, and knowledge migration without overstating ownership of downstream automation outcomes.
